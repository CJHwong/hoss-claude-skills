# Salary Negotiation: Make More Money, Be More Valued

**Source:** https://www.kalzumeus.com/2012/01/23/salary-negotiation/
**Tags:** career
**Saved:** 2024-03-31

## Why It Matters

A $5,000 salary increase compounds over time through raises, bonuses, and 401k contributions - potentially worth $100k+ over a decade. The negotiation itself takes minutes.

## Core Principles

### Understand the employer's math
Employers think in "fully-loaded cost" (150-200% of salary including taxes, benefits, healthcare). A $5,000 difference is ~3% of fully-loaded costs - negligible to them, significant to you. "Bob is not spending Bob's money to hire you. Bob is spending Bob's budget."

### Timing is everything
Only negotiate after achieving "Yes-If" - agreement in principle to hire you if terms work. Never give numbers during interviews or before this threshold.

### Never volunteer a number first
When asked, deflect: discuss mutual fit and value creation instead. If forced, reference total compensation rather than isolated salary.

## Key Tactics

1. **Mirror their language** - Take notes during interviews, identify their stated concerns, address them using their own terminology.
2. **Reveal information strategically** - Present specific value propositions during impasse moments to justify increases.
3. **Trade across dimensions** - Don't fixate on base salary. Negotiate vacation, hours, project assignments, professional development, flexible benefits. Both parties can feel they gained something.
4. **Use non-committal responses** - "Interesting number" (positive but doesn't commit). "The right package could make that attractive" (opens door to creative solutions).

## The Mindset Shift

The article challenges middle-class conditioning against negotiation: "We've turned sucking at it into a perverse badge of virtue." Negotiating signals competence, not rudeness. Companies won't blackball you for negotiating professionally.

## Before You Negotiate

Research: company's compensation structure, career paths for successful employees, corporate culture and flexibility patterns, industry salary ranges.

Knowledge creates options - you can't trade what you don't know exists.
